Main Topics Discussed

  1. Financial Performance and Outlook
    • Review of Q4 2025 results: bookings, revenue, margins, and EPS.
    • Guidance for Q1 and full year 2026, including expected growth rates and FX impacts.
    • Segment performance: B2C, B2B, and advertising businesses.
  2. Strategic Priorities and Initiatives
    • Delivering more value to travelers.
    • Investing in growth opportunities (e.g., AI, product innovation).
    • Driving operating efficiencies and margin expansion.
    • Organizational optimization and cost management.
  3. Technology and Product Development
    • Use of AI for personalization, operational efficiency, and faster property onboarding.
    • Launch of new products (e.g., Cancel for Any Reason assurance).
    • Expansion of ad formats, including video ads on Expedia’s homepage.
  4. Growth and Expansion
    • Expansion of lodging property count and partner-funded promotions.
    • Growth in B2B and advertising businesses.
    • Direct engagement and growth of direct bookings.
    • Announcement of intent to acquire Tiqets to enhance B2B and B2C offerings.
  5. Brand Positioning and Marketing
    • Brand Expedia’s new campaign launched during the Super Bowl.
    • Positioning of Expedia as a one-stop shop, Hotels.com as a hotel pure play, and Vrbo as a vacation rental marketplace.
    • Loyalty program growth and engagement.
  6. Operational Highlights
    • Customer service performance during winter storms and government shutdown.
    • Improvements in site and app speed.
    • Increased participation in promotional events (e.g., Black Friday).
  7. Q&A Session
    • Analyst questions on AI, B2B marketing costs, margin outlook, CapEx, alternative lodging, and customer service.

Key Statements or Decisions

  • Shareholder Returns: Continued opportunistic share repurchases and a 20% increase in quarterly dividend to $0.48 per share.
  • AI and Technology: AI is being leveraged for product development, property onboarding (70% faster), and customer service improvements.
  • Growth Commitments: Ongoing investments in B2B initiatives despite short-term margin pressures, citing 18 quarters of strong double-digit B2B growth.
  • Acquisition: Intent to acquire Tiqets to strengthen B2B and B2C offerings.
  • Brand Campaign: Launch of a new Brand Expedia campaign, achieving over 200 million YouTube views.
  • Operational Excellence: Industry-leading customer service response times during disruptions.
  • Outlook: Q1 2026 bookings growth expected at 10–12% (including 3 points FX tailwind); full-year bookings growth guidance of 6–8%.

Summary of Outcomes

  • Strong Q4 and FY 2025 Performance: Expedia delivered accelerated bookings and revenue growth, expanded margins, and record levels in several key metrics.
  • Strategic Progress: The company advanced its priorities in technology, supply expansion, and customer engagement, with notable gains in B2B and advertising.
  • Operational Improvements: AI-driven efficiencies, faster onboarding, and improved customer service were highlighted as competitive advantages.
  • Shareholder Value: Increased dividend and continued share repurchases signal confidence in ongoing cash generation and growth.
  • Positive Outlook: Guidance for 2026 reflects expectations of continued healthy growth, with investments in technology and B2B expected to drive future performance.
  • Brand and Product Innovation: New campaigns, product launches, and acquisition plans position Expedia for further differentiation and market share gains.
  • Resilience and Adaptability: The company demonstrated agility in responding to external challenges (e.g., AI-driven search changes, weather disruptions) and remains focused on executing its growth strategy.
